# LinkedIn hook patterns (data-backed)

Origin evidence: `swipe/2026-06-25-hook-analysis.md` — a scrape of **189 last-month posts**
from 7 elite creators (Justin Welsh, Jasmin Alić, Lara Acosta, Ruben Hassid, Katelyn Bourgoin,
Allie K. Miller, Magali Dereu), ranked by **real engagement** (reactions + comments + shares). Every
example below is a real top-performing hook with its engagement; steal the **structure**, not the
persona or the slop. Map each onto Sid's substance (AI/agents for music & media; B2B AI adoption).

## What the data actually says (and where it kills common advice)

From the top 60 hooks by engagement, last month:

- **77% are ≤60 characters. Median hook = 9 words / 45 chars.** Short wins. The hook must clear the
  mobile "…see more" fold. The single highest-engagement hook of the month was 9 words; #4 was 4 words.
- **0% are questions.** Not one of the top 60 opened with a question. Don't open with "Ever wondered…?"
- **Only 18% contain a number.** This kills the "always put a number in your hook" advice (and corrects
  our own earlier Magali-only read of ~58%). Numbers are *optional*. When they appear they're a **vivid
  specific** ("a $550,000 job at 38"), never a listicle count ("7 tips").
- **Hook type predicts the *kind* of engagement** (the most useful finding). Pick the hook for the
  reaction you want:
  - **Belief / aphorism → likes + comments** (Justin Welsh owns the top of the board this way).
  - **Identity / vulnerability → comments** (community piles in: Jasmin Alić).
  - **Utility "how to:" / "instead of" → shares + saves** (Ruben Hassid's AI posts: 200–300 shares each).
- **The one-two punch.** Line 1 is a bold claim; line 2 is a short amplifier that widens the loop before
  the fold. "Life is short, man." → "Do everything you want to do." / "Most people are set up for
  failure." → "Their environment is a nightmare."

## The 8 patterns

Each: when to use · the shape · real cited hooks · engagement signature · the Sid/AI adaptation.

### 1. Aphoristic belief (contrarian or permission-giving)
A short, declarative truth-claim about how the world works. Either challenges a norm or gives the reader
permission. The highest-ceiling pattern by raw engagement.
- "Life is more than your work." — Justin Welsh, 10,514 eng
- "The most successful people I know are working less, not more." — Justin Welsh, 7,041
- "You can become incredibly rich and still hate your life." — Justin Welsh, 6,477
- "Most people are set up for failure." — Justin Welsh, 7,343
- Signature: huge likes + comments (people quote-agree and argue).
- **Sid/AI:** "Most AI pilots are built to fail." · "The best AI operator on your team probably can't code." · "Buying another AI tool won't make you faster."

### 2. Identity / vulnerable confession
First-person, emotional, a little exposed. Builds trust and pulls comments.
- "I got fired three times before I turned 28." — Justin Welsh, 4,107
- "My career wasn't always sunshine and rainbows." — Jasmin Alić, 3,409
- "I successfully broke every 'rule' on LinkedIn." — Jasmin Alić, 4,077
- "I'm a proud Bosnian. And I don't hide it." — Jasmin Alić, 3,167
- Signature: comment-heavy (community + "me too").
- **Sid/AI:** "I shipped an AI agent that confidently gave a client the wrong number." · "Two years ago I thought prompt engineering was a fad."

### 3. Curiosity news-drop (open loop)
Announce something intriguing and slightly incomplete; the reader clicks to close the loop.
- "I (secretly) launched a podcast." — Lara Acosta, 3,583
- "I'm leaking my phone number." — Lara Acosta, 2,811
- "I'm hiring! Remote. Competitive Salary. No CV needed." — Lara Acosta, 4,099
- Signature: curiosity-driven reach; high see-more rate.
- **Sid/AI:** "I gave an AI agent access to our whole catalog. Here's what broke first." · "We replaced our reporting deck with one skill."

### 4. Utility "How to [specific result] [in timeframe]:" (colon loop)
A colon that promises a concrete payoff below the fold. The save/share engine.
- "How to use Claude in 25 different ways:" — Ruben Hassid, 3,330 (292 shares)
- "How to climb all 4 layers of Claude in one weekend:" — Ruben Hassid, 3,552 (268 shares)
- Signature: shares + saves dominate (people bank it for later).
- **Sid/AI:** "How to turn one song into 30 pieces of content in an afternoon:" · "How to give a non-technical team an AI agent in a week:"

### 5. Pattern-interrupt "Instead of [familiar default]…"
Name the reader's default behavior, then redirect. Reframes a habit.
- "Instead of watching Netflix tonight." — Ruben Hassid, 3,535 (295 shares)
- "Instead of another YouTube rabbit hole tonight." — Ruben Hassid, 3,468 (297 shares)
- Signature: shares (aspirational utility).
- **Sid/AI:** "Instead of hiring a content team this quarter." · "Instead of paying for another dashboard."

### 6. The callout "You still [outdated behavior]…"
Name where the reader is behind, without insulting them. Creates urgency + identity tension.
- "You still use Claude like in 2025." — Ruben Hassid, 2,899
- "The gap between you and the 'AI guy at work.'" — Ruben Hassid, 2,874
- "You have no experience." — Lara Acosta, 3,405
- Signature: comments + shares (defensive + aspirational).
- **Sid/AI:** "You're still copy-pasting prompts one at a time." · "Your competitor's intern just out-shipped your agency."

### 7. The imperative "Stop [X]."
A two-word command that interrupts. (Deep cut: generic advice claims imperative openers suppress
reach; in this dataset they perform fine — top-30 to mid-pack — when the X is specific.)
- "Stop bookmarking content." — Justin Welsh, 3,061
- "Stop over organizing Claude. It slows you down." — Ruben Hassid, 2,517
- Signature: saves + comments.
- **Sid/AI:** "Stop asking AI for 'a LinkedIn post.'" · "Stop automating the wrong half of the job."

### 8. The striking specific number
The *only* reliably good use of a number in a hook: a vivid, near-unbelievable specific — not a count.
- "I left a $550,000 job at 38 with no plan and very little runway." — Justin Welsh, 5,821
- "Nobody walks away from a $550,000 salary without a really good reason." — Justin Welsh, 4,993
- "100,000+ people have gone through my coaching." (line 2 proof) — Jasmin Alić
- Signature: credibility + curiosity.
- **Sid/AI:** "Our AI artist hit #1 with twice the streams of #2." · "One skill saved a 12-person team a day a week." (only with a real, verified number — never invent one.)

## Cross-cutting rules (apply to every hook)

1. **≤60 characters, one idea.** If it doesn't fit the mobile fold, it's a first paragraph, not a hook.
2. **No questions. No listicle numbers.** Both underperform here. (A *specific* number is fine; a *count* is not.)
3. **Match the pattern to the goal:** want comments → belief/identity; want saves/shares → utility/interrupt.
4. **Write the one-two punch:** line 1 = claim, line 2 = a short amplifier or twist (≤8 words) that deepens the loop.
5. **Concrete beats clever.** A plain, specific line outperforms a wordplay line. Say it out loud.
6. **Steal structure, not persona or slop.** These creators use em-dashes, profanity, and shock that Sid
   doesn't. Voice + anti-slop come from `consulting-copywriting`; substance from `positioning/`. Ruben
   Hassid is the closest direct transfer (AI niche) — study his shapes first for Sid's AI content.
